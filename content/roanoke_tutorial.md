# Roanoke Tutorial — Time Warp: Plymouth or Jamestown

> Pre-game onboarding. 10 slides. Plays before the analyzer hub quiz on first run.
> Voice: first-person present-tense, immersion HARD RULE per §1.5 of the THEME spec.
> Tone: eerie, mysterious, quiet. No comedy. No moralizing. Dignified.
> Skippable on replay via "Skip Tutorial" link on title screen.

---

## Slide 1 — Title / Cold Open

**Image brief:** Foggy Outer Banks coastline at dawn. A small wooden ship anchored in shallow water, sails furled. Period-correct silhouette (no plastic-looking digital art). Low-saturation grays and pale greens. Wikimedia engraving aesthetic acceptable.
**Audio:** Lone wood-flute over distant surf and gulls. Sparse, ominous. No drum (drum is reserved for Native-character leitmotifs per §1.6). Volume 0.5, fading in from 0 over 2 seconds.
**Timeline bar:** Empty bar visible at bottom for the first time. Tick marks for `1587` and `1590` faint. `1590` does NOT yet glow — only the present moment is highlighted (you are arriving in 1590, but you will not see the empty colony until Slide 4).
**Slide text overlay:** Parchment ship's-log card, `IM Fell English` font baked into the PNG.

**Body text:**
> I stand at the rail of a small ship. The fog is thick, and the shore the English call Roanoke rises slowly out of it.
> My name is John White. I am the governor of this colony.
> Three years ago I left my people on this coast to sail home for supplies. Today I have come back.

## Slide 2 — Why We Came

**Image brief:** A period map of the Atlantic with a thin painted line from Plymouth, England across to the Outer Banks. Faded ink, slight tea-stain texture. A small portrait inset of Sir Walter Raleigh in the corner (Wikimedia public domain).
**Audio:** Same flute bed continues. Add a faint quill-on-paper SFX one-shot when the slide loads, as if the log is being written.
**Timeline bar:** `1587` tick illuminates softly. Label appears: "1587 — Roanoke Colony founded."
**Slide text overlay:** Parchment ship's-log card.

**Body text:**
> Sir Walter Raleigh sent us. He has the Queen's charter to plant an English colony in this new land.
> One hundred and fifteen of us came ashore in 1587. Men, women, children. Farmers, carpenters, my own daughter and her newborn girl.
> We are not the first English to try this coast. We are the second. The first attempt failed. We meant to do better.

## Slide 3 — I Sail Home for Supplies

**Image brief:** A small ship under sail, cutting east across a gray sea. White and his crew on deck looking back over the stern at a shrinking shoreline. Painted-engraving style, low saturation.
**Audio:** Flute bed thins. Wind SFX rises slightly. Ship-creak one-shot on slide load.
**Timeline bar:** `1587` tick still glowing. No new tick yet.
**Slide text overlay:** Parchment ship's-log card.

**Body text:**
> The colony needs more than we brought. Seed, tools, salt, nails. The harvest will not be enough.
> The colonists choose me to sail back to England and return with what they need. My daughter and granddaughter stay behind.
> I expect to be gone a few months. I tell them I will come back before the next winter.

## Slide 4 — The War That Held Me

**Image brief:** A wide painted scene of the Spanish Armada — towering galleons, smoke, the English coast in the distance. Public-domain naval painting (there are several on Wikimedia: de Loutherbourg, etc.). Dark and busy after the empty seas of the previous slide.
**Audio:** Flute drops out for one beat. Distant cannon SFX, very faint and far-off — NOT the Jamestown anchor cannon, which is reserved. This is a single muffled boom, low in the mix. Flute returns after the beat, quieter.
**Timeline bar:** A faint, non-highlighted marker at `1588` appears between the ticks — a small ship-icon, no glow. Player learns the bar can hold context, not just their character's events.
**Slide text overlay:** Parchment ship's-log card.

**Body text:**
> I reach England. The country is at war. Spain's Armada is sailing for our coast and the Queen needs every ship that floats.
> No vessel will carry me back to Roanoke. Not this season. Not the next.
> Three years pass before I find a ship willing to cross.

## Slide 5 — The Empty Settlement

**Image brief:** White and a small landing party stepping ashore. Foreground: an open wooden gate hanging crooked, no smoke from any chimney, weeds growing up through a path. Mid-ground: small huts, doors ajar, no movement. Background: low forest and fog. No bodies. No blood. No struggle.
**Audio:** Flute drops to almost silence. Wind picks up, slow and hollow. A single distant gull. Footstep SFX one-shot on slide load — boots on wet sand.
**Timeline bar:** `1590` tick illuminates for the first time, slow pulse. Label appears: "1590 — Roanoke lost." Now the bar holds two highlighted events: 1587 and 1590.
**Slide text overlay:** Parchment ship's-log card.

**Body text:**
> I step off the boat. The wind smells of pine and salt and nothing else. No cookfire. No voices.
> I walk through the gate. The houses are still standing. The walls are still standing. The people are gone.
> No bodies lie in the paths. No graves are dug in the yard. Whatever happened here, it was not a slaughter.

## Slide 6 — The Soft Decision (click-mechanic teach)

**Image brief:** Two paths through the abandoned settlement. Left frame: the shoreline, with sand and waves and the ship offshore. Right frame: the woods past the broken gate, dark with low pines. White stands at the center, looking back and forth.
**Audio:** Flute holds a single low note. Wind underneath. No SFX.
**Timeline bar:** No new tick. Both 1587 and 1590 remain glowing.
**Slide text overlay:** Parchment ship's-log card. This is the soft decision — it teaches the player that buttons can branch, but both options reconverge on the same next slide. No wrong answer.

**Body text:**
> I have to look. Before the daylight goes, I have to look somewhere.
> Do I search the shoreline first, where the boats would have been pulled up — or the treeline, where a path leads inland?

## Slide 7 — The Carving

**Image brief:** A tall wooden post — a fence post or stripped tree — standing alone at the edge of the settlement. Carved into the bark in deep, deliberate letters: **CROATOAN**. Camera close, the rest of the world blurred out. Period-engraving style preferred (Wikimedia has several historical illustrations of the carving — public domain).
**Audio:** Flute swells back in for one phrase, then drops. The wind quiets. A single woodknock SFX one-shot on slide load — like a finger tapping wood.
**Timeline bar:** No new tick. 1587 and 1590 hold their glow.
**Slide text overlay:** Parchment ship's-log card.

**Body text:**
> On a wooden post at the edge of the settlement, someone has carved a single word.
> Deep cuts. Steady hand. No panic in the lettering.
> **CROATOAN.**

## Slide 8 — What It Might Mean

**Image brief:** A rough period-style map of the Outer Banks. Roanoke Island circled. A short distance south, Croatoan Island circled. Hand-drawn arrow between them. The map is sketched into the parchment overlay itself, like a journal page.
**Audio:** Flute returns, quiet and patient. No new SFX.
**Timeline bar:** Holds.
**Slide text overlay:** Parchment ship's-log card with the small map sketch.

**Body text:**
> Croatoan is the name of an island south of here. It is also the name of a tribe who live on that island. We have spoken with them before. They have been friendly to us.
> The carving is not a cry for help. There is no cross beside it — and we agreed, before I sailed, that a cross would be the sign of distress.
> It is a destination. Or it is a place they were taken. I cannot yet tell which.

## Slide 9 — The Storm, and the Mystery

**Image brief:** White's small ship pitching in heavy weather, sails reefed. Black sky, white spray. The Outer Banks shrink behind it through the rain.
**Audio:** Wind rises sharp. Flute is drowned for a beat by storm SFX — heavy rain, rope-strain, distant thunder. Then the flute returns at the end of the slide, quieter than before.
**Timeline bar:** Holds. The 1590 tick stops pulsing — it is now a fixed, solid mark on the bar.
**Slide text overlay:** Parchment ship's-log card.

**Body text:**
> The weather turns hard against us before we can sail to Croatoan. The captain refuses the crossing. We are blown out to sea.
> I never reach the island. I never find my daughter. I never find my granddaughter.
> No one who came after has found them either. To this day, no one knows what happened to the colony at Roanoke.

## Slide 10 — Hand-off to the Hub

**Image brief:** The Outer Banks fading into fog behind the ship's stern. Above the horizon, a faint second silhouette — another ship, much larger, sailing the opposite direction toward the new world. A gull crosses between them. The frame transitions from White's small vessel to a hint of the larger ships to come (Mayflower / Susan Constant silhouettes — keep them faint, almost a ghost).
**Audio:** Flute holds a final long note, then a slow, low string fade-in underneath — the bridge into the hub track. Wind softens.
**Timeline bar:** Both events solid, side by side: `1587 — Roanoke founded` and `1590 — Roanoke lost`. A small label appears beneath the bar for the first time: "Your chronicle. It remembers what you do."
**Slide text overlay:** Parchment ship's-log card. The final card before the hub.

**Body text:**
> The colony is gone. Whatever happened here, England will try again.
> Three years later, no English settlement remains in North America.
> But ships keep sailing.
