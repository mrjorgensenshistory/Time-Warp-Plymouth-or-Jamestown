## Slide 29 — What Really Happened (light teacher voice — outside the immersion HARD RULE, this is the post-game card)

**Image brief:** A clean slide-textbox card on `bg-parchment`. No portrait, no photo — just a chronicle-style card. Small `IM Fell English SC` header.
**Audio:** Piano bed at half volume, looping.
**Timeline bar:** Hidden on this card.
**Slide text overlay:** Slide-textbox card with header "What Really Happened" in `IM Fell English SC`. Body in `Share Tech`.

**Body text:**
> **Bradford lived to age 67.** He governed Plymouth for about 30 of its first 36 years. His manuscript *Of Plymouth Plantation* was lost during the American Revolution, found again in a London library in 1855, and finally returned to Massachusetts in 1897. It is the foundation of almost everything we know about the colony.
>
> **The Mayflower Compact** is widely cited as the first written framework for self-government on what becomes United States soil. It is short — under 200 words — and it does not abolish the King. But it sets a precedent: a community of ordinary people, by their own signatures, agreeing to be bound by laws they together establish.
>
> **The Wampanoag-Pilgrim alliance** held for Massasoit's lifetime. After his death, the treaty frayed. In 1675, his son Metacom — called King Philip — led a war against the New England colonies that killed thousands on both sides. That story belongs to Massasoit's route. It is not Bradford's to tell.
>
> **Plymouth's economy** stayed small. Subsistence farming, fishing, shipbuilding, small artisans. The colony was eventually absorbed into Massachusetts Bay in 1691. The lack of a plantation economy here was structural — short growing season, rocky soil, no cash crop — not a moral choice. The slavery economy will grow further south, where the climate and the crop demanded scale. That story belongs to Rolfe's route.
